SHEET 01 Project Attributes & Constraints

প্রজেক্টের বৈশিষ্ট্য ও সীমাবদ্ধতা
DRAWN BY: PM-101
SCALE: NTS
REV: A

Every project shares six core traits — and is always boxed in by three hard limits. Click a card to flip it.

Six attributes

Unique Purposeনির্দিষ্ট উদ্দেশ্যTAP ⤵
No two projects are alike — each one chases its own one-time goal that hasn't been done in exactly that way before.
Temporaryসাময়িকTAP ⤵
It has a clear start date and a clear end date — once the goal is delivered, the project closes.
Progressive Elaborationপর্যায়ক্রমিক উন্নয়নTAP ⤵
You don't know every detail on day one — the plan gets more specific step by step as work moves forward.
Resourcesরিসোর্সTAP ⤵
Nothing runs on its own — people, money, and tools all have to be supplied to get the work done.
Sponsorস্পন্সরTAP ⤵
Someone pays the bills and sets the direction — that's the sponsor, the project's main backer.
Uncertaintyঅনিশ্চয়তাTAP ⤵
Risks and surprises are part of the deal — plans change because not everything can be predicted up front.

The Triple Constraint

Scopeস্কোপTAP ⤵
The exact list of work that will (and won't) be done. Add work without adjusting this and you get "scope creep."
Timeসময়TAP ⤵
The schedule and deadlines the work must fit inside.
CostবাজেটTAP ⤵
The approved money you're allowed to spend. Push one side of the triangle (scope/time/cost) and the others usually move too.
FIELD NOTEThink of scope, time, and cost as a triangle — squeeze one corner and the other two shift to compensate.

SHEET 03 Success Criteria & Skills

সাফল্যের মানদণ্ড ও দক্ষতা
DRAWN BY: PM-101
SCALE: NTS
REV: A

A project doesn't just need to "finish" — it needs to win on three fronts at once.

On Time & On Budgetসময় ও বাজেটে শেষDelivered the agreed scope, on the original schedule, within the original cost plan.
Happy Customersগ্রাহকের সন্তুষ্টিThe end result is genuinely easy to use and satisfies the client.
Business Valueব্যবসায়িক লাভIt actually saves money, boosts profit, or speeds work up — value beyond just "it shipped."

Hard skills — হার্ড স্কিল

BudgetingEstimate people/software/tool costs and track spending so the money doesn't run dry.
SchedulingList every task, order them sensibly, and map timelines — typically with a Gantt chart.
SDLC KnowledgeKnow your build approaches — step-by-step Waterfall vs. fast, flexible Agile.
ProcurementPlan how you'll buy licenses or bring in outside contractors.
Risk ManagementSpot what could go wrong ahead of time and have a backup plan ready.

Soft skills — সফট স্কিল

LeadershipKeep the team motivated and flex your style to fit the situation.
CommunicationGive clear, simple progress updates so everyone stays aligned.
Conflict ResolutionSettle disagreements between people calmly, before they fester.
Team BuildingTurn a group of different people into one team that actually works together.
Problem-SolvingFind quick, practical fixes the moment something technical breaks.

SHEET 06 Time Management & Dependencies

সময় ব্যবস্থাপনা ও পারস্পরিক নির্ভরতা
DRAWN BY: PM-101
SCALE: NTS
REV: A

Building the schedule runs through six steps — and every pair of tasks relates to each other in one of four ways.

Six scheduling processes

1
Plan Schedule
2
Define Activitiesকাজ চিহ্নিত করা
3
Sequence Activities
4
Estimate Durations
5
Develop Schedule
6
Control Schedule

Four task dependencies — flip for the rule

Finish-to-Start (FS)A শেষ → B শুরুTAP ⤵
B can't start until A is fully done.
[Hardware Inventory] ──► [OS Installation]
Start-to-Start (SS)A শুরু → B শুরুTAP ⤵
B starts the moment A starts — they kick off together.
[UI Design] ⟺ [DB Architecture]
Finish-to-Finish (FF)A শেষ → B শেষTAP ⤵
B can't wrap up until A wraps up too.
[Software Coding] ⟺ [Tech Documentation]
Start-to-Finish (SF)A শুরু → B শেষTAP ⤵
B can only finish once A has started — the rarest dependency type, often used in just-in-time handovers.

অ্যাক্টিভিটি নেটওয়ার্ক ডায়াগ্রাম
DRAWN BY: PM-101
SCALE: NTS

A picture of how tasks chain together — used to spot the longest unavoidable path through the project.

Start
A · Audit2 days
B · Deploy5 days
C · Verify2 days
End9 days total

Key terms — flip for the plain-English version

Forward PassTAP ⤵
Walk left-to-right through the chain to calculate each task's Earliest Start (ES) and Earliest Finish (EF).
Backward PassTAP ⤵
Walk right-to-left from the end date to calculate each task's Latest Start (LS) and Latest Finish (LF).
Slack / FloatTAP ⤵
LS − ES. How many days a task can slip before it delays the whole project.
Critical PathTAP ⤵
The longest chain of tasks with zero slack — delay any task on it, and the whole project slips.

SHEET 08 Cost Management & Cost Types

কস্ট ম্যানেজমেন্ট ও খরচের প্রকারভেদ
DRAWN BY: PM-101
SCALE: NTS
REV: A

Planning, estimating, budgeting, and controlling what the project spends — and learning to label every dollar correctly.

Cash Flow Analysisক্যাশ ফ্লো অ্যানালাইসিসTAP ⤵
Projects yearly costs and gains (and NPV) so leadership doesn't fund too many cash-hungry projects in the same year — also adjusts for inflation.
Tangible Cost/Benefitদৃশ্যমান খরচ ও লাভTAP ⤵
Easy to put a dollar figure on. e.g. a feasibility study that cost a flat $100,000.
Intangible Cost/Benefitঅদৃশ্য খরচ ও লাভTAP ⤵
Hard to price in dollars — goodwill, prestige, or someone's personal unpaid time spent on the project.
Direct Costসরাসরি খরচTAP ⤵
Tied straight to building the product, and the PM controls it — e.g. full-time staff salaries, hardware bought specifically for this project.
Indirect Costপরোক্ষ খরচTAP ⤵
Not tied to one project, and outside the PM's control — e.g. the electricity bill for a 1,000-person office.
Sunk Costসান্ক কস্টTAP ⤵
Money already spent and gone for good. Rule: don't keep funding a failing project just because you've already spent a lot — ignore sunk costs when deciding.
FIELD NOTE"We've already spent $50k, we can't stop now" is the sunk-cost trap — past spending is never a reason on its own to keep going.
